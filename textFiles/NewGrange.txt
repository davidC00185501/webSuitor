Newgrange is a ancient structure that was discovered in County Meath and is approximately 600 years older than the pyramids of Giza.
The building was built primarily from stone and is 12 metres tall and has a diameter of 80 metres. The interior or the building is 
comprised of chambers and hallways featuring carved stones and sculptures and as such is some of the oldest art to be found in europe.  
It is believed that during the times it was used that it served religious purposes and the remains of multiple people were discovered 
in the tomb.
